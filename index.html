I'm Himanshu Bhisikar (pronounced he-mahn-shu), a postgraduate researcher at the Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA), Bengaluru. I am currently working with Dr. Smitha Subramanian wherein we are investigating the presence of Relic galaxies in the local universe using multi-wavelength (SDSS+GALEX) photometric and spectroscopic techniques. Prior to joining IIA, I had graduated from the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER)-Pune, with an Integrated Bachelor's and Master's in Science, with a major in physics and a minor in biology. My MS Thesis was done under the supervision of Dr. Kanak Saha, wherein we studied the properties of the star-forming regions in the nearby Virgo Cluster galaxy NGC 4571.

The objects I am most fascinated by are Galaxies, as they are the building blocks of the universe. There is a wide range of variation observed in their morphologies, evolution pathways, inner structure, components, gas and dust distribution, stellar populations properties, the whole lot. Studying them will give us insights into the history and evolution of our universe.
